lab members

 

Michael Bova is a Biomedical Sciences Candidate conducting thesis work on the human spine. Mike is interested in mechanisms underlying lower back pain and is currently involved in a study examining results of osteopathic manipulative treatment on lateral curvature of the spine. He received his Bachelors degree from Temple University in 2012. His other research efforts include investigating the role of myosin subunits in development of chick muscle as a research technician in Dr. Camille DiLullo’s lab.

Sarah Friedrich is currently pursuing a Masters in Biomedical Sciences and is working on a project examining how varying temperatures and nutrients during early development affects adult vertebral form. She received her Bachelor degree in Anthropology from the University of Florida in 2011. She also had the opportunity to conduct archaeological fieldwork in both southwestern Ethiopia and Mosselbaai, South Africa. She hopes to attend medical school in the near future. 

Michael, Fath M.S. recently completed his Master of Biology from St. Joseph's University. His masters thesis focused on the description of a Mississippian petalodont from the Bear Gulch Limestone of Montana. He is currently working in the Claeson lab using CT scans to study the anatomy of fossil and extant fishes. His general research interests include speciation, morphology, and phylogeny of the vertebrates. 

Sandra Lee is a medical student at PCOM with aspirations of going into either emergency medicine or cardiology.  While her interest in paleontology may initially seem unorthodox, the interest stems from her inherent inquisitiveness about the world around her, and flourished when she took an evolution course during her undergraduate studies at Drexel University.  The current work and research with zebrafish in our lab may provide a clinical aspect that Sandra can apply to her future medical practice.

Trish Mattioli has been a research technician at PCOM since 1995, working with Dr. Camille DiLullo. She joined our lab in 2012 and has started expanding her technical skills to include 3D image processing of computed tomography and MRI datasets of fossil and recent fishes. Trish has a long history of outreach and has supervised dozens of students.
